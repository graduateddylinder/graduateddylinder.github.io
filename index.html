Dylan Hickson

I am a Research Associate in the Center for Wave Phenomena at the Colorado School of Mines in Golden, Colorado. My professional journey as a scientist begain in Toronto, Canada, where I received my PhD in Earth and Space Science from York University's Planetary Exploration Instrumentation Laboratory within the Centre for Research in Earth and Space Science .

Using geophysical and remote sensing techniques to see ever deeper into the natural, and human-made, world fascinates me. In my research, I use such techniques, specifically radar, to improve our ability to study our environment on Earth and beyond.

My research focuses on the electromagnetic properties of rocks and minerals, radar polarimetry and scattering theory, and how radar relates to other wavelengths, such as infrared, optical, and ultraviolet. I strive to improve radar processing algorithms to extract the most information from imaging and sounding observations. Through these studies I investigate the compositions and geomorphology of planetary surfaces as well as processes such as regolith evolution, space weathering, and impact cratering.
